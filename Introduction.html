What is Cryptocurrency?


Cryptocurrency is a revolutionary piece of financial technology that runs on blockchain, a highly advanced tech used for documenting transactions and tracking assets, where anything of value can be traded on, minimizing the risks, and the costs. Serving as digital currency, crypto is an encrypted, decentralized system of digital payments that serve as a medium of exchange.

Crypto being encrypted means it’s able to conceal and protect certain private information by converting it into code that denies any unauthorized access, making it a fairly safe system. In the context of blockchain, decentralization refers to the transfer of power and decision making from a central body, such as a person or an organisation, to a dispersed network.

But why is this fintech(financial technology) called "cryptocurrency": Crypto, means concealed, or encrypted, so it can also be read as "Encrypted-Currency". Crypto refers to the entire concept of this technology, which is the safeguarding of all online payments, such as the encryption codes and cryptographic techniques that builds this piece known as cryptocurrency.













The History of Crypto!


Bitcoin is generally considered the first modern cryptocurrency, since it was the first publicly used medium of exchange that incorporated decentralised control, user confidentiality, blockchain-based tracking, and built-in limitation. Satoshi Nakamoto, an anonymous body of people, initially proposed it in a white paper(report) released in 2008.  Early 2009 is when Satoshi Nakamoto rendered Bitcoin to the general public, and a small core of passionate believers began exchanging and mining the money. By late 2010, the first of dozens of such cryptocurrencies had emerged.

Cryptocurrency's basic roots however go back to the 1980s, when David Chaum, an American cryptographer, founded an algorithm(sequence of intstructions), that is still seen in modern encryption systems. This piece of algorithm enabled parties to exchange unalterable information, setting the framework for future digital currency transactions, such as today's cryptocurrency. Around 15 years later, an engineer who goes by Wei Dai proposed a white paper on an "anonymous, distributed, electronic cash system", called B-money. This sounds a lot like modern crypo and actually had basic components of the technology, such as the encryption and decentralization features.. B-money was referenced by Satoshi Nakamoto when they created bitcoin.
